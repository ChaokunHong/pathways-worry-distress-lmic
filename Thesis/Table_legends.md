# Table legends

## Main tables

**Table 1.** Sample characteristics by country. Descriptive comparison of demographic, socioeconomic, and crisis-context variables across Indonesia (n = 607), Nepal (n = 460), and Vietnam (n = 395). Continuous variables are summarised as median (IQR) or mean (SD) as indicated; categorical variables as n (%). Kruskal–Wallis tests for continuous variables and χ² or Fisher's exact tests for categorical variables.

**Table 2.** Psychometric properties of composite measures. Cronbach's α and McDonald's ω total for the three DASS-21 subscales, the five worry sub-domains, the original 21-item worry composite, behavioural compliance, preventive behaviours, and lifestyle disruption (n complete per scale indicated).

**Table 3.** Correlation matrix (Spearman) among key constructs. Pairwise correlations between the five worry sub-domains, age, education, comorbidity count, livelihood disruption, perceived health threat, self-assessed infection risk, health-system trust, and the three z-standardised DASS-21 outcomes (n = 1,462 after MI pooling).

**Table 4.** Primary multilevel results — MI Rubin-pooled (n = 1,462). Fixed-effect coefficients, 95% confidence intervals, raw p values, FMI, and Benjamini–Hochberg-adjusted p values (15-test pre-specified worry × outcome family) from the primary linear mixed-effects models with random intercept for site, fitted on each of m = 30 MICE imputations and Rubin-pooled. One block per outcome (depression, anxiety, stress); rows include country fixed effects, Block 1 demographics, Block 2 crisis context, and the five worry sub-domains.

**Table 5.** Mediation: indirect effects (complete-case bootstrap comparator). Bias-corrected percentile bootstrap estimates of the five pre-specified indirect pathways (500 bootstraps on complete cases, n = 1,340–1,411 per pathway). Reported as comparator only; see Table S9 for the Rubin-pooled primary mediation analysis.

**Table 6.** Country-stratified regression coefficients (complete-case multilevel; within-country k = 3 / 6 / 4). Fixed-effect estimates from the M3 specification fitted separately within Indonesia, Nepal, and Vietnam. Presented descriptively because each within-country cluster count falls below the Maas–Hox threshold (Note S1); asterisks denote fixed-effect p < 0.05 without family-wise correction across the 45 country × worry × outcome cells.

## Supplementary tables

**Table S1.** Variable construction — items, scoring rules, scale ranges, and missing-data handling for every variable used in the analysis.

**Table S2.** Split-sample worry inventory validation — EFA loadings on the development half (n = 660; maximum-likelihood, oblimin rotation, polychoric correlations; Horn's parallel analysis) and hold-out CFA fit (n = 660; WLSMV; CFI = 0.990, TLI = 0.988, RMSEA = 0.068, SRMR = 0.076).

**Table S3.** Measurement invariance — DASS-21 three-factor model (configural, metric, full scalar, and partial scalar models) tested across Indonesia, Nepal, and Vietnam using MLR estimator.

**Table S4.** Measurement invariance — worry five-factor model with sequential intercept-freeing to establish partial scalar invariance.

**Table S5.** Missing data pattern — complete vs incomplete-case comparison and per-coefficient fraction of missing information (FMI) across MI imputations.

**Table S6.** MI congeniality sensitivity — primary coefficients with site dummies retained in the imputation model compared with the primary MICE specification.

**Table S7.** Variance inflation factors (VIF) for the M3 fixed-effects block.

**Table S8.** Cohen's f² for the worry block (M3 over M2) across the three outcomes.

**Table S9.** Mediation — Rubin-pooled indirect effects across m = 30 imputations using the Schomaker–Heumann bootstrap-after-imputation framework. Five pre-specified pathways.

**Table S10.** Structural equation model — fit indices and standardised structural paths for the five-factor worry specification and the four-factor merged-material-deprivation specification (both WLSMV). Reports empirical under-identification (negative residual variance) in both configurations.

**Table S11.** Exploratory moderation families — community support × worry, stigma contextual decomposition (within/between-site) and stigma × healthcare-worker-in-household, worry × location-type, and gender × worry / age × worry interactions. All reported for transparency; none survived family-wise adjustment.

**Table S12.** Binary DASS-21 caseness — multilevel logistic regression (glmer with site random intercept) using DASS severity thresholds to confirm directional consistency with continuous-outcome findings.

**Table S13.** Attenuation-corrected worry coefficients (β_obs / √reliability) for the primary 15 worry × outcome associations.

**Table S14.** Site-level leave-one-out — maximum |Δβ| for the 15 worry × outcome coefficients when each of the 13 sites is successively excluded.

**Table S15.** Harman's single-factor test (common-method variance diagnostic); reports first-factor variance proportion.

**Table S16.** Age non-linearity — natural cubic spline (df = 4) versus linear comparison via likelihood-ratio test.

**Table S17.** Protocol-fidelity sensitivity — coefficient stability when 38 off-protocol Vietnamese respondents are re-included (14-site sample).
